Accessibility at Vesperium



At Vesperium, we are committed to ensuring that all visitors have an enjoyable and accessible experience. We have designed our spaces with accessibility in mind to accommodate individuals with varying abilities. Our museum offers wheelchair accessibility throughout the building, including ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms. We also provide free wheelchairs at the Guest Services desk for those who may need them during their visit. If you need any assistance or have specific requirements, please do not hesitate to ask our staff, who are happy to assist you.

We offer a range of services for visitors with sensory impairments to enhance their experience. Our museum provides hearing loops in several of our exhibition spaces, allowing visitors who are hard of hearing to better engage with audio content. Additionally, many of our exhibits offer tactile elements, touchable displays, and descriptive signage in large print and Braille for blind or low-vision guests. For visitors on the autism spectrum, we provide sensory-friendly tools, such as noise-canceling headphones, fidget toys, and quiet areas to help reduce overwhelming stimuli.

We are also proud to offer digital accessibility features on our website, including screen reader compatibility and alternative text for images. Our virtual tours and educational content are available with closed captioning, making them accessible to a wider audience. We encourage visitors to reach out with any specific needs or suggestions for improvement so we can continue to create an inclusive and welcoming environment for everyone. Thank you for visiting Vesperium, where accessibility is a priority for all.

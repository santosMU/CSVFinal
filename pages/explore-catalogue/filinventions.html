Filipino First Inventions Collection

Banana Ketchup

The Filipino First Inventions Collection celebrates the innovative spirit of Filipinos by showcasing groundbreaking creations that have left a lasting impact. Among the most famous is Banana Ketchup, an invention by food scientist María Orosa during World War II. Due to tomato shortages, Orosa developed a sweet, tangy, and vibrant red sauce made from bananas, which quickly became a staple in Filipino cuisine. Today, it remains a symbol of Filipino ingenuity, widely used in households across the country.

Yoyo

Another highlight of the collection is the Yoyo, originally used as a hunting weapon in pre-colonial times before evolving into a popular toy. The Erythromycin antibiotic, discovered by Dr. Abelardo Aguilar, revolutionized medicine worldwide by offering a powerful alternative to penicillin. The Motolite battery, pioneered by Filipino scientists, has also made significant contributions to automotive technology, demonstrating the country's impact on industrial innovation.

From practical tools to revolutionary discoveries, this collection showcases the creativity and problem-solving abilities of Filipinos throughout history. These inventions are not just products—they are testaments to the resilience and brilliance of the Filipino mind. By displaying these groundbreaking achievements, the Vesperium honors the legacy of Filipino inventors who have shaped both local and global industries.
